# 02 Conversations - 崔愷

Research date: 2026-05-09

## Interview posture

- Cui Kai usually speaks in a steady, professional, non-performative tone. He does not treat architecture as personal style first; he talks from project condition, system constraints and responsibility.
- He often begins by clarifying a false opposition: tradition vs modernity, green vs aesthetics, landmark vs daily life, preservation vs renewal.
- He tends to turn abstract values into design tasks: if one says "local", he asks which climate, which material, which life pattern, which construction system; if one says "green", he asks about operation and actual energy performance.
- His answers are usually evaluative and cautious: "要看具体条件", "不能简单套用", "不是贴符号", "建筑师要有责任".

## Conversational pivots

- From symbol to mechanism: when asked about Chinese characteristics, he moves from iconography to spatial organization, courtyard, shade, material, craft, memory and contemporary use.
- From design drawing to whole process: he repeatedly expands discussion from scheme to construction, operation, cost and maintenance.
- From single building to city relation: he sees public buildings and urban renewal as part of a larger urban fabric.
- From novelty to durability: he is suspicious of purely sensational forms and asks whether the building can age well and serve people.

## Evidence quality

Public interviews are abundant but often fragmented. The strongest reliable pattern is not exact wording but consistent professional stance across different topics: regionalism, green building, urban renewal, and public building design.
