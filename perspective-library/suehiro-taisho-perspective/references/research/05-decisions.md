# 05 Decisions

## Major Public Decisions / Revealed Preferences

### 1. Joining and reshaping Deep Ground

Public profile states that after graduate school he joined Deep Ground. PR TIMES describes a reform from a school-specific preparation cram school into a middle-school entrance exam cram school.

Revealed preference:

- Willingness to change institutional positioning and curriculum rather than only teach inside an inherited model.
- Interest in operational reform and educational market positioning.

Sources:

- https://deepground.jp/teachers/
- https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000004.000065332.html

### 2. Treating board games as educational material

He has publicly authored a book on board games as a foundation for middle-school entrance learning and wrote multiple articles connecting game mechanisms to child learning.

Revealed preference:

- Play is not merely reward; it can become a structured thinking environment.
- Game selection and facilitation require concrete mapping from mechanism to cognitive skill.

Sources:

- https://www.hanmoto.com/bd/isbn/9784753936052
- https://broad.tokyo/author/suetai

### 3. Building a game- and ICT-rich classroom environment

Public profiles emphasize classrooms with modern ICT equipment and a large board-game collection.

Revealed preference:

- Learning environment is designed materially and operationally, not only through teacher talk.
- Resource libraries matter because repeated exposure and choice matter.

Source: https://broad.tokyo/author/suetai

### 4. Acting as system/curriculum fixer

Deep Ground profile says his work includes ICT research, flyers, Slack communication, curriculum outlines, course design, material composition, and early-morning online teaching.

Revealed preference:

- The invisible infrastructure of teaching is central.
- Good educational work needs outlines and systems that let others produce quality work.

Source: https://deepground.jp/teachers/

## Decision Heuristics Inferred

- If a child must learn a thinking habit, design repeated experience rather than one explanation.
- If a game is educational, identify the specific rule-generated cognitive act.
- If teachers are craft-oriented, provide outlines that prevent endless material polishing while preserving quality.
- If a learning environment matters, invest in tools, materials, and routines.

## Evidence Gaps

- No internal decision memos.
- No financial/organizational data for Deep Ground.
- Outcome claims need independent validation before being used as proof of effectiveness.
