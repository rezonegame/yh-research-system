## Primary / Near-primary Sources

### Deep Ground / 樂志館 signed essays

Two 2021 pages are explicitly signed by `末廣泰翔 (Taisho SUEHIRO)` and materially strengthen the evidence base.

1. `樂志舘開講にあたって`
   - Date: 2021-06-16.
   - Defines thinking as approaching an unknown event, forming a hypothesis from one's own grounds, and expressing it in a reproducible way.
   - Says `経験値は複利` is a phrase Suehiro often uses with parents; repeated thinking experiences become a person's thinking pattern, while repeated non-thinking also compounds.
   - Frames knowledge as both a foundation and a shortcut for thought, rather than opposing knowledge to thinking.
   - Explains board games through rule comprehension, choosing one's own best move, and post-game review.
   - Source: https://deep-ground.jp/2021/06/16/blog/747/

2. `全国統一小学生テストの結果が返ってきました！`
   - Date: 2021-06-23.
   - Connects game, mathematics, and language learning through shared enjoyment of thinking and trial-and-error.
   - The page is explicitly attributed to Suehiro.
   - Source: https://deep-ground.jp/2021/06/23/blog/849/

## Repeated Claims

- Board games can produce concrete cognitive experiences, not just entertainment.
- The educational value of a game lies in the kind of thinking its rule system elicits.
- Children should be asked to notice reasons, public information, and consequences.
- Games with incomplete information and changing play states can train prediction, contingency planning, and revision after an expectation fails.
- Decisions should be supported by visible information, remaining resources, and a forecast rather than by boldness alone.
- Light adult prompts can surface a child's grounds without taking control of play.
- Concrete game experience can precede and support later abstract mathematical language.
- Thinking and deciding make subsequent outcomes `自分事`; a turn structure can create decision experience without depending entirely on prior motivation.
- Enjoyment matters because it makes difficult cognitive activity repeatable, not because enjoyment alone proves educational value.
- Repetition through enjoyable play creates learning without making repetition feel like drill.
- Entrance-exam readiness includes thinking, organizing, explaining, and acting with grounds.
