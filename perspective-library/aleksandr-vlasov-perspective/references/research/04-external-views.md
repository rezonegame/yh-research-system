# 04 - External Views and Criticism

Subject: Aleksandr / Alexander Vlasov
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Supportive external readings

1. CityChangers profile - https://citychangers.org/young-leader-aleksandr-vlasov/
   - Frames Vlasov as a young Russian urban shaper improving former Soviet cities through comfort, heritage, and design.
   - Highlights his work on Vision Zero, tactical urbanism, wayfinding, heritage sign restoration, and public communication.

2. Calameo project publication, Vision Zero: Sokol - https://www.calameo.com/books/006956415c718374c79e4
   - Shows practical application of safety and tactical urbanism ideas to a Moscow district.

3. City Projects / Russian urbanist NGO context - https://city4people.ru/
   - Provides the broader civic-urbanist ecosystem in which Vlasov's work is situated.

## Critical / cautionary views

Because public writing on Vlasov himself is limited, critiques are mostly of the operating context and method:

1. Visual advocacy can be dismissed if not connected to budgets, standards, traffic law, and implementation authority.

2. Tactical urbanism can become symbolic if temporary interventions are not converted into regulation and capital projects.

3. "European-oriented" urbanism can be accused of importing models without fitting Russian climate, governance, housing estates, and mobility conditions.

4. Heritage restoration and wayfinding improve attachment, but cannot solve inequality, weak civil society, or car dependency alone.

5. Vision Zero depends on enforcement, fines, road standards, and political will; design alone cannot fix unsafe driving incentives.

## Pattern from external views

Vlasov's public role is strongest as a translator: he converts international street-safety and liveability principles into visual, local, district-scale proposals for Russian cities. The main risk is implementation gap.

## Extraction notes

The skill should be practical, visual, and hopeful, but must not pretend that renders and maps are enough. It should always ask: who can approve, maintain, enforce, and fund the change?
