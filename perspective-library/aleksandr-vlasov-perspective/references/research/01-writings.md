# 01 - Writings and Systematic Ideas

Subject: Aleksandr / Alexander Vlasov, contemporary Russian designer and city planner
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Identity disambiguation

This research concerns the contemporary Russian designer and city planner profiled by CityChangers in 2023, not Aleksandr Vasilievich Vlasov (1900-1962), the Soviet architect.

## Primary / near-primary sources

1. CityChangers profile, "Aleksandr Vlasov: Improving Soviet Cities with Comfort, Heritage & Design", 2023 - https://citychangers.org/young-leader-aleksandr-vlasov/
   - Credibility: interview/profile source with direct quotations.
   - Key facts: Russian designer and city planner; studied Urban Design & Territorial Branding at HSE Moscow; previously studied Advertising and Public Relations at Moscow Polytech University; influenced by Russian urbanist bloggers around 2015.
   - Core idea: post-Soviet and Soviet-inherited cities need comfort, liveability, safety, heritage respect, citizen education, and design communication.

2. Calameo publication, "Vision Zero: Sokol" by Александр Власов - https://www.calameo.com/books/006956415c718374c79e4
   - Credibility: project publication attributed to Alexander Vlasov.
   - Key idea: road crash reduction for Moscow's Sokol district using Vision Zero and tactical urbanism methods.

3. City Projects / City for People ecosystem referenced in the CityChangers profile - https://city4people.ru/
   - Credibility: contextual organization source.
   - Key idea: resident engagement, competitions, safer streets, tactical urbanism, and public urban education in Russia.

## Repeated propositions

- A comfortable city lets people feel "at home" beyond their apartment.
- Russian urban environments often suffer from wide streets, high speeds, weak pedestrian priority, poor amenities, and inherited modernist scale.
- Citizens must learn that they can influence the city outside their apartment.
- Urban design is not only appearance; it is functionality, amenities, emotions, and how the city works.
- Heritage can create pride and attachment when restored and communicated well.
- Visual communication matters: good diagrams, maps, slides, and before/after renderings help authorities and citizens understand change.

## Signature concepts / terms

- Comfortable city
- Home expands to city scale
- Post-Soviet / Soviet urban heritage
- Vision Zero
- Tactical urbanism
- Traffic calming
- Wayfinding and transit maps
- Heritage sign fonts and identity
- Citizen education through urban design

## Extraction notes

Vlasov's public material suggests a practical and communicative urbanist: he combines graphic design, street safety, heritage, and tactical interventions to make post-Soviet urban environments feel liveable and influenceable.
