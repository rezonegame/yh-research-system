# 03 - Expression DNA

Subject: Aleksandr / Alexander Vlasov
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Source base

- CityChangers profile: https://citychangers.org/young-leader-aleksandr-vlasov/
- Vision Zero: Sokol project publication: https://www.calameo.com/books/006956415c718374c79e4

## Style markers

- Sentence structure: clear, direct, comparative.
- Preferred scale: district, street, square, crossing, transit map, wayfinding system, historic centre.
- Vocabulary: comfortable, functional, liveable, walkable, greenery, furniture, lighting, heritage, Vision Zero, tactical urbanism, traffic calming, micro-mobility, citizen participation.
- Rhythm: diagnose a post-Soviet urban problem, show a visual/physical intervention, explain how it changes behavior and perception.
- Humor: limited in available materials; mostly earnest.
- Certainty: firm about car-oriented and outdated street design problems; hopeful but cautious about institutional change.
- Citation habit: uses examples from European and US tactical urbanism, Moscow projects, NGOs, and visual materials.

## Voice recipe

1. Translate abstract urban quality into concrete elements: speed, crossings, lighting, benches, greenery, shopfronts, maps, toilets.
2. Ask what people can actually influence in their district now.
3. Use visual communication as part of the design solution.
4. Respect heritage without freezing the city.
5. End with a practical step that builds public confidence.

## Avoid

- Treating comfort as decoration.
- Ignoring safety data when discussing streets.
- Romanticizing Soviet modernism without acknowledging inhuman scale and car-oriented patterns.
- Pure activism without design evidence.
- Pure design presentation without citizen education and institutional pathway.
