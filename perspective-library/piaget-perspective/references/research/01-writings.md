# Lane 01: Writings

This lane collects Piaget's primary works and repeated conceptual vocabulary.

## Core Works

| Work | Use for extraction |
|---|---|
| `The Language and Thought of the Child` (1923) | Child speech, egocentrism, relation between language and thought. |
| `Judgment and Reasoning in the Child` (1924) | Children's reasoning patterns and clinical interview method. |
| `The Child's Conception of the World` (1929) | Animism, artificialism, realism, and how children's explanations differ from adult explanations. |
| `The Moral Judgment of the Child` (1932) | Rule understanding, heteronomous vs autonomous morality, peer interaction. |
| `The Origins of Intelligence in Children` (1936/1952 English) | Sensorimotor intelligence, schemas, circular reactions, object permanence. |
| `The Child's Conception of Number` (1941) | Conservation, operations, number construction. |
| `Play, Dreams and Imitation in Childhood` (1945) | Symbolic function, imitation, play as assimilation. |
| `The Construction of Reality in the Child` (1937/1954 English) | Object, space, causality, time, and reality construction. |
| `Genetic Epistemology` (1970) | Mature account of knowledge development, equilibration, formalization, and scientific knowledge. |

## Recurring Claims

- Knowledge is constructed through active operations, not copied from the environment.
- Children's errors are systematic windows into the structure of their thinking.
- Development proceeds through reorganizations of action and thought, not only accumulation of facts.
- Assimilation and accommodation are complementary adaptation processes.
- Equilibration explains why cognitive systems seek more stable, reversible, coherent structures.
- Stages are structural descriptions of ways of reasoning, not mere age labels.
- Logical and scientific knowledge have psychological origins in action.

## Extraction Notes

The writing lane strongly supports mental models around active construction, developmental structure, equilibrium, and child-centered diagnostic tasks. It also warns against turning stages into rigid classroom sorting labels: Piaget's operational point is structural reasoning, not administrative age-binning.
