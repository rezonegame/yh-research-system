# Lane 06: Timeline

## Chronology

| Period | Event / phase | Perspective implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1896 | Born in Neuchatel, Switzerland | Swiss intellectual and scientific context. |
| Childhood and youth | Early interest in biology and natural history | Development as adaptation and organization remains a lifelong analogy. |
| 1918 | Doctorate in natural sciences/zoology | Biological concepts shape later psychology. |
| 1919-1921 | Work/study in Zurich and Paris, including intelligence testing context | Shift toward children's reasoning and clinical method. |
| 1920s | Early child-language, reasoning, and worldview books | Children's thought treated as structurally different, not merely incomplete. |
| 1930s-1940s | Intelligence, reality construction, number, morality, play | Core developmental and operational theory expands across domains. |
| 1950s | International Center for Genetic Epistemology, Geneva | Mature interdisciplinary program connecting psychology, logic, and epistemology. |
| 1960s-1970s | Late summaries and `Genetic Epistemology` | Mature emphasis on origins of scientific knowledge and equilibration. |
| 1980 | Died in Geneva | Legacy becomes foundational but heavily revised by modern developmental science. |

## Phase Changes

- Early biological formation: adaptation and organization.
- Early psychology: child clinical interviews and systematic developmental differences.
- Middle period: construction of intelligence, reality, number, morality.
- Late period: genetic epistemology and interdisciplinary synthesis.

## Stable Through-Line

Across phases, Piaget asks how increasingly stable, reversible, coordinated forms of knowledge emerge from action. That through-line is stronger than any single stage chart.

## Contextual Caveat

Piaget worked before many modern methods in infant cognition, cross-cultural psychology, cognitive neuroscience, and educational intervention research. The perspective should preserve his question-asking power while updating its confidence boundaries.
