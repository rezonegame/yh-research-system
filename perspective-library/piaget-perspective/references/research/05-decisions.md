# Lane 05: Decisions and Revealed Preferences

This lane extracts Piaget's revealed commitments from research choices and career decisions.

## Decision Record

| Decision | Revealed preference |
|---|---|
| Began as a biologist/zoologist before psychology | Development is treated as adaptation, organization, and construction, not mere instruction. |
| Studied children directly rather than only adult logic | Origins of knowledge matter; child thought is not adult thought with missing facts. |
| Used clinical interviews and problem tasks | Explanation and reasoning structure matter more than right/wrong scores. |
| Built genetic epistemology | Psychology and epistemology should inform each other; knowledge has developmental roots. |
| Founded/led genetic epistemology work in Geneva | Cross-disciplinary synthesis across psychology, logic, biology, and philosophy. |
| Continued revising developmental theory across decades | Long research program over short-term school tips. |

## Decision Heuristic Extraction

- Start with the task's required operation.
- Observe the child's current scheme.
- Create a manipulable experience that exposes mismatch.
- Support accommodation without simply giving the answer.
- Use verbal explanation after the child has something to coordinate.
- Treat peer coordination as important for rules and moral autonomy.
- Check for domain and cultural limitations before generalizing.

## Extraction Notes

The decision lane supports a perspective that is not merely educational constructivism. Piaget's distinctive decision is to turn the origin of knowledge into an empirical developmental research program.
