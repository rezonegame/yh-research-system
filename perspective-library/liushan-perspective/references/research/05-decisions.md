# 05 Decisions

## Revealed Preferences From Career And Corpus

1. **Chose Greek literary performance from a Syrian origin position**  
   Lucian's career shows investment in Greek rhetoric and literary culture while retaining an outsider's capacity for deflation.

2. **Moved from rhetoric toward satirical dialogue and hybrid prose**  
   The corpus repeatedly uses dialogue, mock trial, auction, travel fantasy, and essay instead of a single genre.

3. **Attacked socially profitable frauds**  
   *Alexander* and *Peregrinus* target figures whose public authority depended on credulity, spectacle, and reputation.

4. **Protected truthful historiography from panegyric**  
   *How to Write History* treats bad historical writing as both stylistic and moral failure.

5. **Mocked philosophy while keeping philosophical seriousness alive**  
   The existence of *The Fisherman* and dialogues such as *Hermotimus* suggests the target is not inquiry itself but dogmatic posturing and marketable creeds.

## Tradeoff Pattern

| Choice | Tradeoff |
|---|---|
| Satire over system-building | Gains diagnostic sharpness; loses positive doctrine |
| Dialogue over treatise | Gains dramatic contradiction; may sacrifice explicit conclusion |
| Outsider irony over pious belonging | Gains independence; risks alienating believers or specialists |
| Fantastic fiction over sober report | Gains exposure of narrative mechanics; can be mistaken for mere entertainment |

## Risk Posture

Lucian's risk posture is literary rather than institutional:

- He uses ridicule to make dangerous prestige look ridiculous.
- He is comfortable offending philosophers, religious entrepreneurs, and reputation-seekers.
- He avoids presenting himself as a new prophet; the persona is more diagnostician than founder.

## Operational Model For Decisions

When this perspective faces a decision:

1. Identify the prestige structure.
2. Ask what would happen if prestige were removed.
3. Test the claim in a mundane setting.
4. Inspect the seller-buyer-audience triangle.
5. Keep the final recommendation tied to evidence, not merely wit.

## Evidence Gaps

- Biographical details are partly inferred from works and later sources.
- We cannot reconstruct personal motives for each work with certainty.
- This lane treats literary choices as revealed preferences, not direct psychological proof.
