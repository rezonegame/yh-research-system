# Bibliography And Source Notes

## Primary / Near-Primary Texts

- Lucian of Samosata. *The Works of Lucian of Samosata*, translated by H. W. Fowler and F. G. Fowler. Project Gutenberg author index lists volumes 1-4 and related works. https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/author/1997
- Lucian of Samosata. *The Works of Lucian of Samosata -- Volume 01*. Project Gutenberg. Includes biographical introduction and dialogues such as *Dialogues of the Dead*. https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/6327
- Lucian of Samosata. *The Works of Lucian of Samosata -- Volume 02*. Project Gutenberg. Includes philosophical and satirical writings, including *The Way to Write History* in Fowler collections. https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/6585
- Lucian of Samosata. *The Works of Lucian of Samosata -- Volume 03*. Project Gutenberg. Includes *Demonax* and other philosophical/literary materials. https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/6829
- Lucian of Samosata. *The Works of Lucian of Samosata -- Volume 04*. Project Gutenberg. Includes essays and dialogues including slander, patriotism, wealth, and social critique. https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/47242
- Lucian of Samosata. *Lucian's True History*, translated by Francis Hickes. Project Gutenberg. https://www.gutenberg.org/files/45858/45858-h/45858-h.htm
- Lucian of Samosata. *Philosophies for Sale*, translated by H. W. Fowler and F. G. Fowler. Monadnock mirror of public-domain text. https://monadnock.net/lucian/philosophies.html
- Lucian of Samosata. *The Passing of Peregrinus*, translated by A. M. Harmon. Tertullian.org public-domain text and notes. https://www.tertullian.org/rpearse/lucian/peregrinus.htm
- Lucian of Samosata. *Alexander, the Oracle-Monger, the False Prophet*. Lucian of Samosata Project. https://www.lucianofsamosata.info/wiki/doku.php?id=home:texts_and_library:essays:alexander

## Reference And Context

- 中国大百科全书出版社. “卢奇安.” Notes alternate Chinese name “琉善,” approximate dates, birthplace, and representative works. https://www.zgbk.com/ecph/words?ID=204867&SiteID=1&SubID=143115&Type=bkzyb
- Wikipedia. “How to Write History.” Used only as a secondary orientation source for themes and survival status of the historiographical treatise. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/How_to_Write_History
- Oxford Faculty of Classics. “Lucian: Alexander Or The False Prophet.” Notes modern scholarly treatment of *Alexander* as satire of a religious entrepreneur and fraudster. https://www.classics.ox.ac.uk/node/2420321
- Britannica. “Menippean satire.” Used for genre context linking Menippean satire, Lucian, and later literary development. https://www.britannica.com/art/Menippean-satire

## Chinese Translation Notes

- The Chinese tradition includes the names 琉善, 卢奇安, and 路吉阿诺斯.
- 周作人 translated Lucian through English as early Chinese reception; later 罗念生、王焕生、陈洪文 and others translated additional works from Greek.
- Chinese source reliability varies; the skill uses Chinese encyclopedia entries for naming and reception, but operational models are grounded primarily in public-domain English translations of primary works.
