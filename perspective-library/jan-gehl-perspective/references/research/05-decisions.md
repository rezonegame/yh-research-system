# 05 - Decisions and Actions

Subject: Jan Gehl
Research date: 2026-05-09

## Public actions / professional choices

1. Shifted architectural attention from buildings as objects to life between buildings.
   - Source: Gehl company story and PPS biography - https://www.gehlpeople.com/company/ ; https://www.pps.org/article/jgehl
   - Decision logic: study what architecture does to human behavior, not only what it looks like.

2. Long collaboration with Copenhagen's pedestrianization and public-space transformation.
   - Source: widely documented in Gehl profiles and city-planning histories.
   - Decision logic: reclaim streets incrementally, observe outcomes, and normalize walking/cycling/public life.

3. Founded Gehl Architects / Gehl as a practice translating public life research into city consulting.
   - Source: Gehl firm profile - https://www.gehlpeople.com/company/
   - Decision logic: make public-life measurement a professional service, not just an academic argument.

4. Extended the method internationally: Melbourne, New York, Moscow, London, Sydney, and other city projects are often associated with Gehl's firm and methodology.
   - Source: Gehl project materials and public accounts.
   - Decision logic: export the method, then adapt to local street culture, climate, and governance.

## Decision heuristics inferred

- If a space has no optional activities, its social life will remain thin.
- If a design looks good from above but feels poor at walking speed, fix the ground-level design first.
- If people do not linger, the city has not truly invited them.
- If the data only counts cars, the design will serve cars.
- If a plan cannot be tested through observation, it is probably too abstract.

## Tensions

- Gehl's work is practical and observable, but the political conditions for implementation are not always under a designer's control.
- His method favors incremental public-realm transformation; some crises require structural policy decisions beyond urban design.
