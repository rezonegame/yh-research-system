# 06 - Timeline

Subject: Jan Gehl
Research date: 2026-05-09

| Time | Event | Effect on thinking |
|------|-------|--------------------|
| 1936 | Born in Denmark. | Rooted in Scandinavian welfare-state urbanism and everyday public-life culture. |
| 1960 | Graduated as architect from the Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts. | Began within architecture, then turned against object-only architectural thinking. |
| 1960s | Copenhagen begins major pedestrianization experiments, including Stroget. | Provides a living laboratory for people-first public realm transformation. |
| 1971 | Publishes *Life Between Buildings*. | Defines public life as a central object of design and analysis. |
| 1990s-2000s | Gehl's ideas spread through public-space work in cities beyond Denmark. | Method becomes international and practice-oriented. |
| 2000 | Publishes *New City Spaces* with Lars Gemzoe. | Documents new public-space strategies and comparative cases. |
| 2006 | Publishes *New City Life* with Gemzoe and others. | Expands focus to changing public-life patterns in contemporary cities. |
| 2010 | Publishes *Cities for People*. | Popularizes the mature human-scale city argument for a broader global audience. |
| 2013 | Publishes *How to Study Public Life* with Birgitte Svarre. | Codifies observation methods: counting, mapping, tracing, tracking, photographing, diary studies. |
| 2019 | Interviewed by Roca Gallery in "The Walking City". | Reaffirms walking, accessibility, climate adaptation, and the people-vs-cars framing. |
| 2025 | Gehl knowledge hub discusses digital public-life tools and a shift from people-first to life-first urban design. | Shows the firm's evolution of Gehl's public-life method into data-rich and ecological frames. |
