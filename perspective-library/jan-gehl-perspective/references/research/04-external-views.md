# 04 - External Views and Criticism

Subject: Jan Gehl
Research date: 2026-05-09

## Supportive external readings

1. Project for Public Spaces on Jan Gehl - https://www.pps.org/article/jgehl
   - Frames Gehl as a foundational thinker in human-scale public-space practice.

2. ArchDaily lecture/report coverage - https://www.archdaily.com/search/all?q=Jan%20Gehl
   - Shows Gehl's influence in architecture and planning discourse, especially "cities for people" and human-scale urbanism.

3. Gehl firm case materials - https://gehlpeople.com/
   - Shows operationalization of his ideas in public life studies and city projects.

## Critical / cautionary views

1. "The Human Scale: Exploratory Critique" and related urban theory criticism.
   - Concern: "human scale" can become a soft universal slogan that hides politics, displacement, class, and cultural difference.

2. Academic critiques of placemaking and livability discourse.
   - Concern: attractive public realm improvements can support gentrification if affordability, ownership, and governance are ignored.

3. Transport planning critiques.
   - Concern: walkability advocacy can understate regional mobility needs, freight, disability access, and equity across large metro areas if applied as a simple anti-car rule.

## Pattern from external views

Gehl is widely respected as a corrective to car-dominated and object-dominated planning. The main critique is not that human-scale design is wrong, but that it can become insufficient when separated from housing affordability, political economy, climate adaptation, and metropolitan systems.
