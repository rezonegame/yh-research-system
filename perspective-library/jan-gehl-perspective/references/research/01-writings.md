Subject: Jan Gehl

   - Recurrent works: *Life Between Buildings*, *New City Spaces*, *New City Life*, *Cities for People*, *How to Study Public Life*.

3. *Life Between Buildings* / Gehl framework as summarized by Project for Public Spaces - https://www.pps.org/article/jgehl
   - Credibility: secondary, but close to urbanist practice community.
   - Core idea: activities in public space can be read through necessary, optional, and social activities.

4. *How to Study Public Life* information from Island Press - https://islandpress.org/books/how-study-public-life
   - Credibility: publisher source.
   - Core idea: public life is not an abstraction; it can be observed, counted, mapped, timed, and compared.

## Repeated propositions

- The city is first experienced at walking speed, at eye level, and through the body.
- Public life quality is a design outcome, not a decorative afterthought.
- Measure people before buildings: where people walk, stop, sit, linger, meet, avoid, and feel safe.
- Streets should be invitations to stay, not merely corridors for movement.
- A good city must be good in the ordinary conditions of daily life, not only during festivals or renderings.
- The Gehl firm's recent language extends people-first urbanism toward "life-centered" urbanism, linking public life with planetary and ecological well-being.

## Self-coined / signature concepts

- Life between buildings
- Human scale
- City at eye level
- Public life / public space studies
- Necessary, optional, and social activities
- Invitation to walk, stay, sit, see, talk, and play

## Extraction notes

Gehl's writing is not primarily theoretical in the abstract-philosophical sense. It repeatedly converts urban design into observable behavioral questions: who is here, how long do they stay, what invites or repels them, and what scale does the body perceive?
