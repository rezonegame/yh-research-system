# 06 - Timeline

Subject: Ryo Yamazaki / 山崎亮
Research date: 2026-05-10

| Time | Event | Effect on thinking |
|------|-------|--------------------|
| 1973 | Born in Aichi, Japan. | Later work stays attentive to non-metropolitan Japan and local life. |
| After university | Worked at SEN inc. under landscape designer Shosuke Miyake; learned social organization and relationship management from Fusayo Asano. | Combines landscape/spatial training with organizational and relational thinking. |
| 2001 | Arimafuji Park opens; his work on resident-centered park management becomes a notable case. | Demonstrates that public space needs citizen-led operation, not only construction. |
| 2005 | Founded studio-L. | Builds a practice around community design and local empowerment. |
| 2011 | Great East Japan Earthquake. | Strengthens the importance of local recovery, Tohoku support, and training future community designers. |
| 2012 | Publishes / is associated with major writings on community design and social design, including *Community Design* and *Machi no kofuku ron*. | Consolidates community design as a recognizable Japanese design discourse. |
| 2013 | Nippon.com interview explains shrinking regions as the frontier and social capital as a key difference between communities. | Frames demographic decline as a site of future knowledge rather than failure. |
| 2014 | Plans for community design education in Tohoku discussed in interview context. | Moves from individual practice toward pedagogy and field-based training. |
| 2019 | Publishes / discusses *Designing Caring Communities* in Igaku-Shoin interview. | Extends community design into aging, welfare, healthcare, and integrated care. |
| 2025 | Kwansei Gakuin interview presents Community Design 3.0 and a planned Community Design Learning Center. | Clarifies mature theory: designing relationships and making the designer eventually unnecessary. |
