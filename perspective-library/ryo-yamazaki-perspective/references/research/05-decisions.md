# 05 - Decisions and Actions

Subject: Ryo Yamazaki / 山崎亮
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Public actions / professional choices

1. Founded studio-L in 2005.
   - Source: studio-L profile - https://studio-l.org/en/members/yamazaki.html
   - Decision logic: create a professional platform for community design work beyond conventional landscape/building design.

2. Shifted from hard infrastructure to soft process.
   - Source: Nippon.com and studio-L profile.
   - Decision logic: in shrinking communities, the important design work is enabling residents to solve problems through relationships and ideas.

3. Park management at Arimafuji Park.
   - Source: Keio MCC lecture report - https://www.keiomcc.com/magazine/sekigaku137/
   - Decision logic: a public park needs active citizen-led programs and "cast" roles, not only maintenance. The number of participating groups grew from 22 in the opening year to 75 by 2013 in the report.

4. Work on community-based integrated care.
   - Source: Igaku-Shoin interview - https://www.igaku-shoin.co.jp/paper/archive/y2019/PA03315_01
   - Decision logic: care is not only professional coordination; it also requires resident participation and everyday relationships.

5. Education and next-generation training.
   - Source: Nippon.com 2013 interview and Kwansei Gakuin 2025 interview.
   - Decision logic: community design should spread as a capability, not remain dependent on one specialist.

6. Community Design Learning Center planned in 2025.
   - Source: Kwansei Gakuin 2025 interview.
   - Decision logic: build a practical learning base for adults and professionals to learn "relationship design" through immersive workshops.

## Decision heuristics inferred

- If residents will not own the process, the expert should slow down and redesign participation.
- If a plan cannot survive after the consultant leaves, it is not yet community design.
- If the same people always attend, search for absent residents and easier entry points.
- If social capital is stuck, use an outside facilitator to slightly reorganize relationships.
- If the proposal is correct but nobody moves, add sensory, enjoyable, or pride-generating triggers.
- If a workshop only collects opinions, it has not yet created agency.

## Tensions

- He wants residents to become central, but the first step often depends on an external facilitator.
- He rejects hard-infrastructure-first thinking, yet many projects still involve buildings, parks, hospitals, libraries, and public facilities.
- He values social capital, but also knows it can be a fetter when local relationships are conflicted.
