# 04 - External Views and Criticism

Subject: Ryo Yamazaki / 山崎亮
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Critical / cautionary views

Public critical material is less concentrated around Yamazaki personally, but the method faces recurring risks:

1. Participation fatigue
   - Repeated workshops can burden the same motivated residents and leave quieter groups out.

2. Soft process without hard authority
   - Relationship design can generate ideas but still depend on budgets, legal authority, facility management, and political decisions.

3. Outsider facilitation paradox
   - An outside designer may help reorganize stuck relationships, but can also unintentionally set the agenda or become too central.

4. Community romanticism
   - Social capital can be exclusionary, hierarchical, or conflict-ridden. "Local ties" are not always benign.

5. Local revitalization as depoliticization
   - Focusing on resident self-help may underplay structural causes such as fiscal decline, national policy, labor markets, and care shortages.

## Pattern from external views

The method is respected because it refuses both top-down facility building and one-off consultation. The main caution is to avoid turning empowerment into a polite word for shifting responsibility from institutions to residents.

## Extraction notes

The skill should preserve Yamazaki's optimism about latent local agency while keeping an honest boundary around governance, inequality, and burnout.
