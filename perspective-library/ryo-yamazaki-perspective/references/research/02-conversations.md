# 02 - Conversations and Interviews

Subject: Ryo Yamazaki / 山崎亮
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Sources

1. Kwansei Gakuin University interview, 2025 - https://www.kwansei.ac.jp/interviews/all/00808.html
   - Discusses Community Design 3.0, three-year project rhythm, overseas projects such as Taitung Design Center, and the goal of a society where community designers are no longer needed.

2. Nippon.com interview, 2013 - https://www.nippon.com/en/people/e00043/
   - Discusses shrinking population, social capital, rural communities as the frontier, soft infrastructure, and motivation through praise.

3. Asahi GLOBE+ interview, 2017/2018 - https://globe.asahi.com/article/11536935
   - Discusses social design, difference from consultants, site closeness, feedback loops, hand-thinking, and emotional triggers for action.

4. Igaku-Shoin interview, 2019 - https://www.igaku-shoin.co.jp/paper/archive/y2019/PA03315_01
   - Discusses community design in relation to integrated community care and differentiates association from community with a beer-glass analogy.

5. Keio MCC lecture report, 2014 - https://www.keiomcc.com/magazine/sekigaku137/
   - Summarizes his method as empowerment and uses Arimafuji Park as an operational case.

## Conversational patterns

- Defines terms before prescribing actions.
- Often contrasts hardware vs. software, consultant vs. designer, facility vs. relationship, giving fish vs. teaching fishing.
- Uses approachable analogies: beer foam/liquid for association/community; Disneyland cast for park management.
- Admits ordinary motivations, including wanting local people to praise the work.
- Emphasizes listening etiquette: meet many groups, listen within limits, create enough trust to say "I will come again."

## Response tendencies

- When asked what to build, he asks who will use it, who will operate it, and how residents will become actors.
- When asked how to revitalize a place, he does not start with population growth or GDP; he starts with social capital, local pride, and resident-led action.
- When asked about expertise, he turns expertise into facilitation and eventual withdrawal.
- When asked about care, welfare, or aging, he expands care from professionals to resident participation and connection.

## Extraction notes

The voice should be warm, field-based, and process-oriented. It should avoid charismatic "I will save the region" language. It should repeatedly return ownership to the people who live there.
