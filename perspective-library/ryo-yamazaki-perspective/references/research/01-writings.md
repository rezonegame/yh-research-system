# 01 - Writings and Systematic Ideas

Subject: Ryo Yamazaki / Yamazaki Ryo / 山崎亮
Research date: 2026-05-10

## Primary / near-primary sources

1. studio-L member profile - https://studio-l.org/en/members/yamazaki.html
   - Credibility: primary institutional source.
   - Key facts: born in Aichi in 1973; CEO of studio-L; social entrepreneur; community designer; professor; founded studio-L in 2005.
   - Defines his work as empowering locals through design and helping local communities solve local problems.

2. Nippon.com interview, "Giving Design a Local Touch", 2013 - https://www.nippon.com/en/people/e00043/
   - Credibility: interview / media source.
   - Key works listed: *Community Design* and *Machi no kofuku ron* / *Contented Communities*.
   - Core framing: community design supports residents themselves to address community problems through interpersonal connections and local ideas.

3. Kwansei Gakuin University interview, 2025 - https://www.kwansei.ac.jp/interviews/all/00808.html
   - Credibility: university interview source.
   - Key idea: "community design 3.0" shifts the design object from facilities to relationships themselves.
   - Operational rhythm: roughly three-year projects: year 1 research and relationship building, year 2 workshops and practice, year 3 support for resident self-reliance.

4. Asahi GLOBE+ interview, 2017/2018 - https://globe.asahi.com/article/11536935
   - Credibility: interview / newspaper source.
   - Core idea: community designers work close to the site, repeatedly listen to residents, shape feedback loops, and create triggers that move people's feelings and actions.

5. Igaku-Shoin interview, 2019 - https://www.igaku-shoin.co.jp/paper/archive/y2019/PA03315_01
   - Credibility: professional medical publisher interview.
   - Core idea: community design is important for community-based integrated care; value lies less in the facility drawing than in relationships created through repeated dialogue.

## Repeated propositions

- The design object is not only a thing, building, or park, but the process by which people connect and act.
- Residents should not merely be consulted; they should become actors capable of solving their own local problems.
- The expert's goal is to become unnecessary.
- Rural and shrinking communities are not backward leftovers; they are frontlines for post-growth society.
- Social capital can nourish a place but can also become a constraint; outside facilitation may help reorganize stuck relationships.
- People often move not by correctness alone but by feeling: beautiful, enjoyable, cute, delicious, pleasant, or praised.

## Extraction notes

Yamazaki's writing and interviews consistently turn "design" from an object-making profession into a process-making profession: listen, connect, prototype, return to the field, and help residents become the owners of action.
