# 05 Decisions - Yusuke Kakei / 筧裕介

Research date: 2026-05-09

## Key decisions and logic

- 1998: Joined Hakuhodo and worked in advertising/marketing/design. Later treated advertising knowledge as a way to make social initiatives reach people. Confidence: high. Source: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/
- 2001: Witnessed 9/11 in New York. He describes the experience as changing how he saw society and opening the thought that design could work at a larger social scale than advertising. Confidence: high. Sources: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/ and https://www.businessinsider.jp/article/2510-kakei-yusuke-2/
- 2008: Founded issue+design while connected to graduate research in social design. Decision logic: move from commercial communication to applying design to public/social problems. Confidence: high. Sources: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/ and https://www.anow.jp/business/14/
- Disaster design: `できますゼッケン` translates volunteer capacity into a visible wearable signal. Decision logic: solve a coordination problem by making hidden capability legible. Confidence: high. Source: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/
- Dementia project: began from `認知症未来共創ハブ` and chose the first-person perspective of people with dementia because he was not a medical expert and found a gap in available materials. Confidence: high. Source: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/
- SDGs/decarbonization programs: use games and facilitation to let local participants experience system interdependence. Decision logic: people learn systemic thinking better through embodied play than through lectures. Confidence: high. Sources: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/ and https://business-chronicle.com/person/issueplusdesign.php
- 2025/2026: `認知症世界の歩き方` expands toward film and international projects after books/NHK/translation impact. Decision logic: if book reaches only already-concerned audiences, use another medium to reach people who still feel unrelated. Confidence: high. Source: https://issueplusdesign.jp/dementia_world/movie/

## Decision pattern

Encounter a credible stakeholder -> listen for invisible lived logic -> translate the issue into a beautiful/fun/shared artifact -> build participation around it -> accept that structural change is slow and iterative.
