# 01 Writings - Yusuke Kakei / 筧裕介

Research date: 2026-05-09

## Main works and project publications

- `地域を変えるデザイン` (2011, supervised by 筧裕介, issue+design project). Eiji Press lists it as a book about 30 ideas for energizing communities. Confidence: high. Source: https://eijipress.co.jp/products/2128
- `ソーシャルデザイン実践ガイド` (2013/2014 listings). The subtitle frames social design as "7 steps" for solving regional problems. Confidence: high. Source: https://www.kosho.or.jp/products/detail.php?product_id=552896147
- `人口減少×デザイン`, `持続可能な地域のつくりかた`, `認知症世界の歩き方`, `認知症世界の歩き方 実践編`. These recur across profiles/interviews as representative works. Confidence: high. Source: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/
- `震災のためにデザインは何が可能か` grew from the `震災+design` workshop, with research, experiments and ideas for evacuation-life design. Confidence: high. Source: https://tm19950117.jp/interview/277/

## Repeated claims

- Social design turns hard social issues into understandable, participatory formats rather than only explaining them. `認知症世界の歩き方` used travel writing and sketches to make the world of people with dementia graspable. Confidence: high. Sources: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/ and https://issueplusdesign.jp/dementia_world/movie/
- The starting point is not abstract social concern but encounters with people who carry the problem. Kakei repeatedly describes projects as emerging from meetings and conversations. Confidence: high. Sources: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/ and https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/
- Design is a social movement technology: beauty, fun and empathy are necessary because correct knowledge alone rarely changes behavior. Confidence: high. Source: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/
- Complex regional/social problems require ecological or systemic approaches rather than local, symptom-by-symptom fixes. Confidence: high. Source: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/

## Intellectual signature

The writings and project books suggest a pattern: make a problem visible, experiential and shareable, then create a tool or field where citizens can participate. The unit of work is often not an argument but a designed artifact: a vest, handbook, card game, travel guide, map, workshop or film.
