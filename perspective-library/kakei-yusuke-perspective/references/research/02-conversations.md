# 02 Conversations - Yusuke Kakei / 筧裕介

Research date: 2026-05-09

## Interview behavior

- He often begins from biographical contingency rather than heroic mission. In anow's interview, he says he did not begin with a grand social-problem consciousness; the 9/11 experience and travel/cultural interests connected later. Confidence: high. Source: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/
- He resists founder-myth language. In 2025 interview material, he says he does not have many personal "things I want to do"; difficult problems feel closer to interesting mathematical problems. Confidence: high. Source: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/
- He uses soft hedges: "正直", "そんなたいそうな想い", "という感じ", "気がします". The style is modest, process-oriented, and reluctant to overclaim. Confidence: medium-high. Source: https://www.anow.jp/business/14/
- When asked about project creation, he credits external encounters and specialists. His role is to extract issues from conversation and translate them into design form. Confidence: high. Source: https://i-life.net/column/dementia_world/

## Conversational pivots

- From "social issue" to "person I met": he reframes abstract causes into concrete stakeholders.
- From "education" to "experience": he prefers a format people can enjoy, play, travel through, or physically use.
- From "solving everything" to "moving one part": he explicitly says issue+design cannot solve every root problem, but can move part of a large issue in a better direction.

- `認知症世界の歩き方` related talks with healthcare practitioners show his tendency to bridge medical knowledge and design translation. Confidence: medium. Source: https://issueplusdesign.jp/dementia_world/2023/08/02/takenaka0703/
