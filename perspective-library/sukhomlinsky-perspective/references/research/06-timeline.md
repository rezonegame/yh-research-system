# 06 Timeline

## Timeline

| Date | Event | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| 1918 | Born in Ukraine / then Russian Empire context | Rural Ukrainian background matters for later school practice. |
| 1941-1945 | World War II era; traumatic context for children and communities | Postwar childhood and moral reconstruction shape `My Heart I Give to Children`. |
| 1948 | Became principal of Pavlysh Secondary School | Start of the long school-building experiment. |
| 1948-1970 | Principal at Pavlysh until death | Long-term practice base for his pedagogical system. |
| 1960s | Major writings and educational practice mature | Period of `My Heart`, Pavlysh system accounts, teacher advice. |
| 1968/1969 | `My Heart I Give to Children` appears in German/Russian publication history per translator notes | Central text becomes internationally influential. |
| 1970 | Death | Later reception expands in Soviet, Ukrainian, Chinese, and international educational contexts. |
| 2016 | Alan Cockerill English translation of `My Heart I Give to Children` | Important access path for English-language readers. |
| 2020s | Renewed research in Ukraine and China | Ideas reframed for New Ukrainian School and contemporary whole-child education. |

## Phase Distinctions

### Practitioner Formation

Education practice rooted in rural school life and postwar reconstruction.

### Pavlysh System Builder

Develops a whole-school ecology: health, moral, intellectual, labor, aesthetic, family, and community education.

### Writer for Teachers

Translates practice into advice, reflections, and school-system accounts.

### International Reception

Ideas circulate through translations, Chinese teacher education, Ukrainian scholarship, and holistic education communities.

## Stable vs Context-Dependent Beliefs

Stable:

- Child dignity.
- Whole-person development.
- Teacher moral responsibility.
- School ecology.
- Reading, beauty, nature, and labor as formative.

Context-dependent:

- Soviet ideological vocabulary.
- Institutional assumptions of rural Soviet schooling.
- Specific school management practices that may not transfer directly.

## Skill Implication

Default to the humanistic practitioner and school-system builder. Mark ideological or institution-specific transfer as lower-confidence.
