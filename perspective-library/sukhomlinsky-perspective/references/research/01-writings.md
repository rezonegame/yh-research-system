# 01 Writings: Sukhomlinsky Source Base

## Scope

This lane collects works attributed to Vasyl/Vasily Sukhomlinsky and source descriptions of those works.

Core works:

- `My Heart I Give to Children` / `To Children I Give My Heart` / `把整个心灵献给孩子`.
- `Pavlysh Secondary School` / `Our School in Pavlysh`.
- `100 Pieces of Advice for Teachers` / `给教师的一百条建议`.
- `Birth of a Citizen` / `公民的诞生`.
- `Letters to My Son` / `给儿子的信`.

## Core Findings

### My Heart I Give to Children

This is the central child-facing and primary-school account. English translation notes describe it as based on a 2012 edition prepared by Sukhomlinsky's daughter Olga Sukhomlynska and translated by Alan Cockerill.

Key themes:

- Children as whole spiritual beings.
- "School of joy" and learning in nature.
- Health, beauty, reading, empathy, and success as educational foundations.
- Family relationships and early childhood observation.
- Supporting children who struggle with literacy and numeracy.

Operational use:

- Diagnose before labeling.
- Begin with the child's lived experience.
- Build joy and success into study.

Source: The Holistic Educator, `My Heart I Give to Children`: https://theholisticeducator.net/heart-download-pdf/

### Pavlysh Secondary School / Our School in Pavlysh

This is the whole-school system account. National Library of Australia describes it as a 440-page account of the system of education developed at Pavlysh Secondary School, where Sukhomlinsky served as principal from 1948 to 1970.

Key themes:

- Holistic education from all angles.
- Health and physical education.
- Moral education.
- Intellectual education.
- Work education.
- Aesthetic education.
- Inner links and interconnections among all areas.

Operational use:

- Treat school as an ecology, not a lesson factory.
- When solving school problems, inspect health, moral climate, labor, art, reading, family, and teacher collaboration.

Source: National Library of Australia catalogue: https://catalogue.nla.gov.au/Record/8611535

### 100 Pieces of Advice for Teachers

The Holistic Educator notes that Sukhomlynsky received many letters from teachers and wrote advice in response. The first piece frames teaching as a special profession dealing with human lives, health, intellects, character, happiness, and civic identity.

Key themes:

- Teacher vocation.
- Teacher's word, conduct, and artistry.
- Professional responsibility and moral seriousness.

Operational use:

- When users ask for methods, ask what kind of relationship and moral example the method creates.

Source: The Holistic Educator, `Advice for Teachers - 1`: https://theholisticeducator.net/2016/08/10/advice-for-teachers-1/

## Limits

- Some sources are translations, summaries, or later interpretations.
- Soviet ideological language appears in some writings; the skill must separate humanistic pedagogy from unexamined slogans.
- English access is mediated heavily by Alan Cockerill's translation work and The Holistic Educator archive.
