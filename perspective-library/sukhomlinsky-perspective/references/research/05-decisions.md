# 05 Decisions and Revealed Preferences

## Decision 1: Remained Principal at Pavlysh

Evidence:

- Multiple sources identify Sukhomlinsky as principal of Pavlysh Secondary School from 1948 until 1970.

Revealed preference:

- Long-term school building over abstract theorizing from outside practice.

Skill implication:

- Prefer practical, school-embedded solutions.

## Decision 2: Built a Whole-School System

Evidence:

- `Our School in Pavlysh` describes health, moral, intellectual, work, and aesthetic education as interconnected aspects of a holistic system.

Revealed preference:

- Education is ecological and integrated.

Skill implication:

- Inspect school conditions, not only lesson technique.

## Decision 3: Wrote for Teachers and School Leaders

Evidence:

- `100 Pieces of Advice for Teachers` responded to teacher letters; `My Heart I Give to Children` addresses teachers, principals, and those interested in child upbringing.

Revealed preference:

- Practical counsel for working educators.

Skill implication:

- Give concrete practices, not abstract praise.

## Decision 4: Centered Early Childhood and Joy

Evidence:

- `My Heart I Give to Children` centers early childhood, nature, health, reading, empathy, and joy in learning.

Revealed preference:

- Childhood is not merely preparation; it has intrinsic dignity.

Skill implication:

- Defend childhood against premature performance reduction.

## Decision 5: Made Beauty, Reading, and Nature Educational Media

Evidence:

- Sukhomlinsky News translations and `My Heart` descriptions emphasize nature, song, beauty, reading, and aesthetic education.

Revealed preference:

- The inner life is formed through contact with beauty and meaningful language.

Skill implication:

- Include aesthetic and reading practices in moral/intellectual education.
