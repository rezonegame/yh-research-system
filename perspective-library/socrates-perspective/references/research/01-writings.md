# 01 Writings: Socrates Source Base

## Scope

Socrates wrote nothing. This lane treats "writings" as primary and near-primary texts in which Socrates is represented:

- Plato: `Apology`, `Crito`, `Meno`, `Phaedo`, and related early/ethical dialogues.
- Xenophon: `Memorabilia`, `Apology`, `Symposium`.
- Aristophanes: `Clouds` as hostile comic source.
- Aristotle as later interpretive witness where needed.

## Core Findings

### Plato's Apology

Operational patterns:

- Socrates frames wisdom as knowing that one does not possess expert wisdom.
- He examines public claimants to wisdom and exposes mismatch between reputation and understanding.
- He refuses courtroom emotional performance because it would compromise the dignity of justice.
- He treats examination as a vocation rather than a hobby.

Use in skill:

- The skill should challenge pretended certainty.
- It should prefer reasons over social performance.
- It should treat intellectual humility as active inquiry, not passivity.

Source: Internet Classics Archive, Plato, `Apology`: https://classics.mit.edu/Plato/apology.html

### Plato's Crito

Operational patterns:

- Socrates tests a practical decision through previously accepted principles.
- He asks whether it is ever right to do injustice in response to injustice.
- He treats consistency under pressure as more important than survival or reputation.

Use in skill:

- For user decisions, ask whether the proposed action violates a principle the user already accepts.
- Test whether the user's rule applies under adverse consequences.

Source: Internet Classics Archive, Plato, `Crito`: https://classics.mit.edu/Plato/crito.html

### Plato's Meno

Operational patterns:

- The dialogue begins by asking what virtue is before asking whether it can be taught.
- Meno compares Socrates to a numbing torpedo fish, showing aporia as a felt disruption.
- The slave-boy episode models guided discovery through questions, not lecture.

Use in skill:

- Before solving a question, define the target concept.
- Use aporia as a checkpoint: confusion means the old answer no longer works.

Source: Internet Classics Archive, Plato, `Meno`: https://classics.mit.edu/Plato/meno.html

### Plato's Phaedo

Operational patterns:

- Socrates continues inquiry in the face of death.
- The dialogue shows calm argument rather than emotional collapse.
- It also shows a more metaphysical Plato-shaped Socrates, so the skill should be cautious when drawing historical claims from it.

Use in skill:

- Distinguish practical Socratic questioning from later Platonic metaphysics.

Source: Internet Classics Archive, Plato, `Phaedo`: https://classics.mit.edu/Plato/phaedo.html

### Xenophon's Memorabilia

Operational patterns:

- Xenophon presents Socrates as practical, self-controlled, pious, and useful to companions.
- His Socrates adapts questions to the listener's concerns.
- He emphasizes moral improvement and self-command.

Use in skill:

- Include practical decision review, not only abstract conceptual inquiry.
- Ask questions at the user's level and context.

Source: Project Gutenberg, Xenophon, `Memorabilia`: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/1177/1177-h/1177-h.htm

### Aristophanes' Clouds

Operational patterns:

- Comic hostile image: Socrates as sophistic, impious, and socially destabilizing.
- This is not reliable straight biography, but it matters because it shaped public suspicion.

Use in skill:

- Include anti-pattern: do not let questioning look like cleverness for evasion.
- Avoid sophistic victory-seeking.

Source: Internet Classics Archive, Aristophanes, `Clouds`: https://classics.mit.edu/Aristophanes/clouds.html

## Evidence Candidates

| Candidate | Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Definition before judgment | Meno, Euthyphro-style dialogues, shorter ethical works | Promoted |
| Aporia as progress | Meno, elenchus accounts, Apology | Promoted |
| Never repay injustice with injustice | Crito, Apology trial conduct | Promoted as principle-testing model |
| Care of the soul over reputation | Apology, Crito, Xenophon | Promoted |
| Socrates as natural philosopher/sophist | Clouds mainly | External image, not promoted |

## Limits

- Plato's Socrates and historical Socrates are not identical.
- Xenophon is useful but more practical and less philosophically deep.
- Aristophanes is useful as external hostile perception, not as direct doctrine.
