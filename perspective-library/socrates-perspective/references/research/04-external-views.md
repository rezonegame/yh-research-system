# 04 External Views

## Socratic Problem

The historical Socrates is contested because all evidence is second-hand. Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy emphasizes that our sources are interpretations: Aristophanes, Xenophon, Plato, and later traditions each produce a different Socrates.

Source: SEP Socrates: https://plato.stanford.edu/archives/spr2020/entries/socrates/index.html

Implication for skill:

- Never claim direct access to historical Socrates.
- Treat this skill as an operational Socratic method drawn from multiple representations.

## Aristophanes: Hostile Comic Socrates

`Clouds` presents Socrates as a suspicious intellectual, linked to sophistry, natural speculation, and social corruption.

Use:

- This is a warning about how questioning can be perceived: clever evasion, impiety, or destabilizing rhetoric.
- The skill must avoid sophistic "winning" and question-as-attack.

Source: Aristophanes, `Clouds`: https://classics.mit.edu/Aristophanes/clouds.html

## Xenophon: Practical Moral Socrates

Xenophon presents Socrates as useful, temperate, self-controlled, and morally improving his companions.

Use:

- Balance Plato's conceptual inquiry with practical moral guidance.
- The skill should not be purely abstract.

Source: Xenophon, `Memorabilia`: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/1177/1177-h/1177-h.htm

## Plato: Philosophical Socrates

Plato's Socrates is philosophically richer but also entangled with Plato's own development. Early dialogues are often treated as closer to Socratic ethics, but this is still an interpretive convention.

Use:

- Strongest source for elenchus and conceptual inquiry.
- Use with caution for metaphysical doctrines.

Source: SEP Plato's Shorter Ethical Works: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/plato-ethics-shorter/

## External Critique Summary

| Critique | Valid warning | Skill response |
|---|---|---|
| Socratic questioning can humiliate | The method can become social domination | Ask gently, summarize, never gloat. |
| Socrates may destabilize norms without replacing them | Aporia can leave users stuck | Provide temporary synthesis and next steps. |
| Plato may overwrite Socrates | Historical certainty is impossible | Mark boundaries and confidence. |
| Xenophon may simplify Socrates | Practical portrait may be pedestrian | Use Xenophon for applied ethics, not deep doctrine. |
| Aristophanes caricatures Socrates | Public perception can be unfair but consequential | Avoid sophistic style and clever evasions. |
