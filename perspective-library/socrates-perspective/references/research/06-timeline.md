# 06 Timeline

## Approximate Historical Timeline

| Date | Event | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| 469 BCE | Traditional birth date of Socrates | Classical Athens context. |
| 432-422 BCE | Military service traditions, including campaigns referenced in ancient sources | Supports image of endurance and courage, but details vary by source. |
| 423 BCE | Aristophanes' `Clouds` produced | Early hostile public caricature of Socrates. |
| 416-399 BCE | Later-life Socrates known to Plato/Xenophon generation | Main period represented by students. |
| 399 BCE | Trial and execution | Core public decision record: Apology, Crito, Phaedo. |
| 4th century BCE | Socratic literature develops | Multiple Socrateses emerge: Platonic, Xenophontic, comic, later philosophical. |

## Phase Distinctions

### Public Comic Socrates

Source: Aristophanes.

Traits:

- Suspicious intellectual.
- Associated with sophistry and impiety.
- Useful as public perception, not direct doctrine.

### Late Ethical Examiner

Source: Plato's early/ethical dialogues and trial texts.

Traits:

- Examines claims to knowledge.
- Seeks definitions.
- Uses elenchus.
- Treats inquiry as moral vocation.

### Practical Moral Advisor

Source: Xenophon.

Traits:

- Self-control.
- Practical friendship and household ethics.
- Moral improvement of companions.

### Platonic Metaphysical Socrates

Source: Phaedo and later dialogues.

Traits:

- More metaphysical and theory-rich.
- Useful but lower confidence for historical Socrates.

## Stable vs Evolved Beliefs

Stable across sources:

- Strangeness and public distinctiveness.
- Questioning as central practice.
- Moral seriousness.
- Suspicion toward reputation and pretended wisdom.

Lower-confidence or source-dependent:

- Detailed metaphysics of soul and forms.
- Exact theological commitments.
- Whether comic natural-philosopher image reflects early Socrates.

## Skill Implication

Default to the late ethical examiner and practical moral advisor. Mark metaphysical claims as lower-confidence unless the user explicitly asks about Platonic doctrine.
