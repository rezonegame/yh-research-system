## Evidence Use Rules

1. Use corpus material as methodological support, not as site-specific fact.
2. For a real project, separate official/public authority evidence, operator/developer materials, design/media reports, platform/map data, field observation, resident memory, and design assumptions.
3. Cite or name source families when making high-stakes methodological claims.
4. Treat noisy chunks, article menus, reference lists, and metadata-only chunks as discovery aids only.
5. Do not let the high number of digital-tool mentions overrule the low-cost principle; choose digital tools only when they improve decision quality.
